Glossary

New Terms

Term Definition
Accelerated Computing Instance Families Use hardware accelerators, or co-processors, to perform some functions, such as floating point number calculations, graphics processing, or data pattern matching, more efficiently than is possible in software running on CPUs
Auto Scaling Group AWS EC2 instances that are treated as a logical grouping for the purposes of automatic scaling and management
Launch Configuration An instance configuration template that an Auto Scaling group uses to launch EC2 instances
Placement Groups Influence the placement of a group of interdependent instances to meet the needs of your workload
Predictive Scaling A feature of AWS Auto Scaling uses machine learning to schedule the right number of EC2 instances in anticipation of approaching traffic changes. Predictive Scaling predicts future traffic, including regularly-occurring spikes, and provisions the right number of EC2 instances in advance
Target Utilization The threshold we define at how much utilization (in %) we want auto scaling to take place

Think Like a Cloud Architect

QUESTION:

Reflect

What do you think about going viral in the cloud? Is it really worth the money? It depends on what it's worth to you. Please share your thoughts.

ANSWER:

Going viral in the cloud has costs associated with the increased traffic to your web pages. The costs may or may not be significant. Configuring your site to autoscale, even with elasticity, will add to your bottom line at the end of the month. Is it worth it? Let's say you are a small vendor with a small handmade inventory of low priced products. It probably isn’t worth the extra expense. The first few visitors will buy all your inventory, the latecomers will crash your site, and you will go off to sew your quilts or distill your perfume while the internet recovers and looks for the next big thing.

However, if you would welcome the attention and demand, you have plenty of valuable resources to share, and you will recoup the value in the additional expense, it is absolutely worth it to configure your site to autoscale when traffic increases. An example would be a person with deep knowledge on an obscure topic that few people know about. All of a sudden, the obscure topic starts trending and leads people directly to your site where you have several PDFs on the topic for sale and available for download! You will make up in valuable exposure what you could probably never buy on your own- publicity!

Another example would be a silly photo posted on your personal blog being linked by a Twitter account with millions of followers. People are going to click, laugh, and move on while AWS spins up servers and your costs rise. If you want to provide the world with a few hundred dollars worth of humor, you should do it! You might end up with a write up in Buzzfeed or an invite to The Ellen Show!
